## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the process for handling data subject rights requests under the Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023 and enforced by the National Data Protection Commission (NDPC). It ensures that all requests are handled consistently, promptly, and in compliance with regulatory requirements.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all personal data held by the organisation and all staff who handle data subject requests.

---

## 3. Data Subject Rights Under NDPA 2023

| Right | Description |
|---|---|
| Right to Access | Data subject can request a copy of their personal data |
| Right to Correction | Data subject can request correction of inaccurate data |
| Right to Deletion | Data subject can request deletion of their personal data |
| Right to Portability | Data subject can request data in a portable format |
| Right to Withdraw Consent | Data subject can withdraw consent at any time |
| Right to Object | Data subject can object to certain types of processing |

---

## 5. Identity Verification

Before processing any request the organisation must verify the identity of the data subject to prevent unauthorised disclosure.

Accepted verification methods:
- Confirmation of account email and registered details
- Government issued ID for sensitive requests
- Security questions where applicable

---

## 6. Response Timeframes

| Request Type | Response Timeframe |
|---|---|
| Acknowledgement | Within 3 business days |
| Full response | Within 30 days of receipt |
| Complex requests | Up to 60 days with notification to data subject |

---

## 7. Handling Each Request Type

### 7.1 Right to Access

1. Verify identity of the data subject
2. Locate all personal data held for that individual
3. Compile a data subject access report
4. Deliver the report securely within 30 days
5. Log the request and response in the request register

### 7.2 Right to Correction

1. Verify identity of the data subject
2. Review the inaccurate data identified
3. Correct the data across all systems
4. Notify any third parties who received the inaccurate data
5. Confirm correction to the data subject in writing

### 7.3 Right to Deletion

1. Verify identity of the data subject
2. Assess whether deletion is legally permissible
3. If permissible delete data from all systems
4. Notify third party processors to delete the data
5. Confirm deletion to the data subject in writing
6. Document any data retained and the legal basis for retention

### 7.4 Right to Portability

1. Verify identity of the data subject
2. Compile the data in a structured machine readable format
3. Deliver securely to the data subject within 30 days
