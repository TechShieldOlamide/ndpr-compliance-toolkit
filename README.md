## What is the NDPA?

The Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) was signed into law in June 2023 and is enforced by the National Data Protection Commission (NDPC). It replaces the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) 2019 as the primary data protection legislation in Nigeria. The NDPA governs how organisations collect, process, store, and share personal data of Nigerian citizens. Non-compliance attracts fines of up to 2% of annual gross revenue or 10 million naira whichever is higher.

## Key NDPA Requirements

1. Obtain lawful consent before collecting personal data
2. Publish a clear and accessible privacy policy
3. Appoint a Data Protection Officer if processing large volumes of data
4. Report data breaches to NDPC within 72 hours
5. Conduct a Data Protection Impact Assessment for high risk processing
6. Engage only NDPA compliant data processors
7. Register with the NDPC as a data controller or processor where required
